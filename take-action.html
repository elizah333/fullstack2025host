WHERE WE WORK

WWF works on jaguar conservation across Latin America, from the Amazon rainforest and the Pantanal wetlands to the dry forests of Central America. These landscapes are not only vital for jaguars but also home to millions of people and countless other species that depend on healthy ecosystems.

Out of these key landscapes, several are priority restoration areas where WWF and partners are working with governments and local communities to reconnect fragmented habitats, secure migration corridors, and bring jaguars back to areas where they have disappeared.

WWF also tackles wider threats beyond these landscapes — from the illegal wildlife trade and poaching, to deforestation, infrastructure development, and conflicts with humans and livestock.

Adopt a Jaguar

The three jaguars who symbolically represent our adoptions were among the first jaguars ever photographed by our camera traps, and like all wild jaguars, they led solitary and elusive lives. Their descendants are among the last jaguars to freely roam South America today.

When you adopt Luna, Kael, or Sombra you’re making a symbolic but incredibly powerful gesture to protect the rainforest their descendants now rely on for survival. By adopting one jaguar, you’re helping to safeguard this small but critical population and actively protect their habitat against poachers, logging, and illegal encroachment.

Your adoption will start working immediately, supporting vigilant Wildlife Protection Units to patrol protected habitats day and night, deterring poachers, untangling snares, and guarding against illegal logging. Just as important, your protection extends to all native species, making sure the jaguars under your protection have a steady source of prey.

Jaguars are elusive predators that live mostly solitary lives. Their needs can only be met in the wild, in their natural environment. This is the goal of our Jaguar Adoption Program. Thank you for being a part of it.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I support Jaguar conservation?

You can support by spreading awareness, donating to conservation programs, or making sustainable choises that reduce deforestation in Jaguar habitats.

Can I volunteer to help Jaguars?

Yes, some organizations offer volunteer programs in jaguar range countries. Volunteers may assist with research, habitat restoration, and community education.

Is it possible to adopt a Jaguar?

You can symbolically adopt a jaguar through reputable conservation organizations. Your adoption helps fund protection of jaguars and their habitats.

Where does my donation go?

Donations support anti-poaching patrols, habitat restoration, research, and education programs that help safeguard jaguars and biodiversity.

Can I make a monthly donation?

Yes, many conservation groups offer monthly donation options that provide stable, long-term support for jaguar projects.

How do community programs help jaguars?

Community programs teach sustainable farming and provide alternatives to deforestation, helping people and jaguars live side by side.

Can schools get involved in jaguar conservation?

Schools can organize awareness campaigns, fundraisers, or use jaguar education resources to teach students about conservation.

What other ways can I take action?

Besides donating or volunteering, you can reduce your ecological footprint, support sustainable brands, and advocate for stronger wildlife protection policies.
